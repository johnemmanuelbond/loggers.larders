## YF: Founding Years
Life Begins on Melian, an Earth-like world.

After 2 billion years and some mass extinctions, the continents are more or less modern.
### Merling Civilization
The sea peoples, [[Ancient Merlings]], are the first civilization to crop up on Melian. Utilizing shamanic rituals to summon and trap spirits from beyond the shaded door, they invent shadebinding, but their watery home disincentivizes blood magic and it never takes root in their culture.

The ruins below [[Capital City 2]] were once the beating heart of this ancient, world-spanning empire.
### Humans Evolve
Early along in the Merling civilization, humans evolve on the continent of [[Sundwythe]]. To them, the advanced Merlings seem like terrible, baby-snatching gods.

Over this time the humans integrate into merling society and spread over the world.

While they start as a lower caste of predominantly laborers and servants, they eventually become more like partners to their Merling overlords.
### The Arrival of Elves
Elves arrive from another world on their swan ships, fleeing the malice of [[Pan]].

These elves claim the continent pf [[Nordwythe]] and repel the Merling civilization using their blood magic.

The human servants they liberate eventually become the [[Aire Nomads]].
### The Arrival of Dwarves
The dwarves arrive through fiery portals into the cavernous halls beneath the [[Mountains of Melian]]. Where they stay, isolated, for thousands of years.
### Pan Arrives
The true god [[Pan]] arrives to Melian as the gas giant planet Panain.

Melian becomes tidally locked.

The immense tidal forces deform Melian to an ellipsoid, and massive volcanic eruptions wrack the world.

The Merling civilization is destroyed and their remnants flee into the deep oceans. The surface world is made toxic to merlings due to the volcanic minerals spewed forth.

Human development is sent back centuries, and most of the ancient history is lost.

The elves retreat deep into the forest.

The dwarves are forced to surface for a time.

The world is forever split into cultures who see Pan's disc, and those who don't.
## TB: The Blooming of Peoples
Modern nation states arise and fall in the rubble of the former merling empires.

Most nations fall to corruption, slavery, and other evils.

Secritive sects of blood mages spring up and breed a plethora of slave races through unholy ceremonies.

More secretive mages/clerics summon Pan's demons into the world where they inhabit fell shapes.

Yet more secretive mages/clerics rediscover the shaded door.
