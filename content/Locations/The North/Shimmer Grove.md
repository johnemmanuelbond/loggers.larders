#### Entering Shimmer Grove:
Following the steady southward trail of this stream leads you out and around the final twisting turn of this gulley. The tall mountainous walls on either side begin to fall back and a true wide valley unfolds before you.

Dark stone faces dark with runoff from yesterdays storm greet you from the south, west, and east. At this moment you realize the path you've taken is perhaps the only way in or out of this place as the mountain ridges on every other side are taller, starker, and more foreboding than the ground you've already traversed.

Carrying the scent of wet stone, pine resin, and clean fresh water, the breeze in this valley gently rustles the leaves and needles of the forest spilling down the mountainside. Deep green waves of pine and fir, broken up here and there by the warm colors of this early autumn. Even now a few of the trees have started to change into the rich patches of amber, copper and crimson that you know will soon engulf these slopes.

While you indeed sit in the lowliest of the surrounding mounts, you still look down upon the valley floor, swallowed beneath layers of canopy and drifting mist. Occasional clearings in the treeline dot the landscape, containing  rocky outcroppings of stone pushing through earth or beneficent meadows of underbrush swaying in the wind.

Looking out at this vista your mind is drawn back to that faint sheen on the air, like sunlight reflecting off of rippling waves. Your mind is brought to the gleaming white tree you saw in the astrolabe, that glowing haze in the southern sky, and the pinpoint of light emanating from Val pointing you south.

###### If they look toward the western woods

The western side of the valley is dense with old-growth forest.

The egarthopian trees grow tall and proud, close packed into a forest brimming with dark needles which eat up the light. Occasional deciduous trees begin their blaze of autumn colors, but they only serve to highlight the scale of the woodland. Judging by the shape of the canopy you expect the terrain beneath to be uneven--ridges streambeds, and outcroppings of old eroded stone--but most of the woodland remains inscrutable from this distance.

***High Perception:***  
*Through a narrow gap in the trees, you catch the glint of moving water winding between the hills below. While significantly larger, and faster moving, than this measly stream the river is not large. From here you can't gauge where it's flowing.*

###### If they look toward the eastern slopes

You follow that faint whish on the air to the southern slopes. The peaks on the eastern side of the valley are fewer than the north, but higher than the west. Their faces are marked by old fractures and the the tumultuous paths of landslides. Sparse trees cling stubbornly to the higher elevations while the lower slopes retain the general verdance of the rest of the valley.

***High Nature:***  
*Listening closer you notice that that whoosh on the air sounds a lot like rushing water, and the relative sparsity of peaks in this location indicates that there might just be a route for a river to flow between the mountains.*

###### If they focus on the valley floor

The valley floor appears to be uninterrupted forest, but under further scrutiny you discern a pattern in the forest coverage as it gradually thickens further south. The longer you stare you begin to pick up on some clues: faint breaks in the canopy, subtle shifts in color and height of the trees, grey wisps of mist or smoke rising between the trees. This valley does not appear totally *wild* to you.


***High Perception:***  

*Two things in particular pique your curiosity: Near the northern edge of the valley, deliberately concealed beneath towering pines, you glimpse what looks like a circle of tall hide tents. South and east from there, near the middle of the valley, there is another shape — larger, older, built of pale stone barely distinguishable from the rocky hill it sits upon.*

###### If they focus on the heart of Shimmer Grove

Val is pointing directly at the southeast corner of the valley. There you see a crescent shape in the hill line, nearly secluding a smaller grotto from the main valley. The forest there is thick and uninterrupted canopy of green leafy trees, dark and older than the surrounding pine trees. Most drifts slowly amongst the tops of the trees and beyond that you catch the occasional familiar ripple in the air.

***High Perception:***  
*For just a moment, the wind parts the upper canopy in the far distance and you glimpse pale white leaves amongst the trees there. Its branches stretch wide and tall like a vaulting cathedral. Then the branches shift again, and it vanishes from sight.*

#### Preserved Ash
In the middle of Shimmer Grove stands a mighty ash tree. It'srough textured brown bark is regularly grooved and peckled with shadows, which causes the light to wash out when it hits the tree and makes it look almost white. Starting just after Sole's eve, and continuing through the end of November this tree's leaves start disapparating, rather than falling, into rising speckless of dull white light rising into the sky.
