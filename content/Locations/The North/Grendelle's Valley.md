#### Sealed Tomb
The party comes across a hill surrounded by a decrepit stone wall, upon a successful check they discover one side of the hill is in fact a boulder covered in overgrowth, sealing the entrance to a mausoleum.

If the party succeeds at a skill challenge, they can open the sealed cave, which inside holds a tombstone and some shelves. Ceramic vases line the shelves, and a mural portraying an ancient battle in the forest adorns back wall: blue-skinned warriors with scaley armor battle against a column of [[Northern Dwarves|dwarvish]] soldiers--who seem to be retreating following a cabal of tall figures in flowing robes.
- A DC 23 history check from [[Bartholomood|Bart]] yields nothing, suspiciously, the dwarves never wrote of this war.
- A DC 23 history check from an [[Andradorans|Andradoran]] will bring knowledge of similarly scaled armor in ancient frescas across [[Mardwythe]].

Inside the vases are a treasured of silver coins with the image of a trident, but opening the vases or disturbing the entombed warrior will summon a spirt who can curse those who open it and seal the boulder behind them. Only dust remains of the entombed warrior, but inside the burial is an empty suit of scaly armor, a trident, and a shield, all magical.
- The scale mail armor grants a swim speed equal to your walking speed, and negates the disadvantage on stealth checks.
- The shield is +1.
- And the trident is +1, finesse, and when struck with a metal object rings like a tuning fork, deafening and charming enemies in a cone. The charmed enemies cannot move, take actions, or reactions, but break the charm (not the deafen).

#### Newt Tunnels
These tunnels vary between 5 and 10 feet in diameter. At times two medium creatures can walk side by side. The walls are thick packed mud and silt held together by a crystalized sort of clear amber compound (newt mucus).

Some tunnels are totally submerged, requiring a DC 10 athletics check to swim through, a failure results in getting tired and caught on the rocks. (reminder, PCs can hold their breath for 1+con minutes). Somewhere there is a wrecked raft with... (unvisited)

Per hour of time spent there's a cumulative 10% chance to run into 2 hungry lake newts (who can totally be bought off with food). There are 4 main caverns on the map. Each cavern has a chance to hold newts, glowworms, or nothing. Slaying the glowworms reveals the coin of flipping.
- Caverns 1 (small pop.) and 4 (larger pop). contain some Glamourspores (recognizable on a DC 15 nature check) which are a magical component for several works of illusion magic. On their own, each cap can be used as up to 50gp worth of spell components for illusion spells, potion brewers also make extensive use of them for invisibility potions, among other things.
- Cavern 2 is the lake newt den and sits at a nexus of tunnels.
- Cavern 3 is very small but has a hole leading down to a larger room. Inside this room there's an elven skeleton wearing a signet right (everything else is rotted away). A DC 11 survival check picks up on the flowing water, which will eventually lead to an exit at the waterfall that heads off Shae's Stream at the sealed tomb. Otherwise a compass works to lead east.
