###### Parent: [[Harport]]
Mostly built up quickly after the big fire of 0125, the resulting neighborhood has basically no internal structure. Here we see a combination of two-story tenements, poorly demarcated hostels, family homes, [[Aire Nomads|Aire]] lean-tos, thatch huts, and a series of winding paths and lanes which connect the [[Guild Quarter]] to [[The Citadel]]. At the south end of Birdfoot, at the border of the [[Harport|Ag Sector]], looms the enormous racetrack, which doubles as a gambling den, and the best spot in town to broker deals. Next to the racetrack is Harport's red light district, complete with a series of bars and clubs right across the river from the Citadel. In fact [[Harport City Watch|watchpersons]] are this district's  most consistent clientele. While it's not obvious from the surface, the underlying limestone on the Birdfoot is ideal for delving secure underground shelters, and so the population here is even larger than expected.

### Tunnels
Carved out of the limestone rock that is the Birdfoot peninsula, the interior tunnels are home to about a third of the population of the district itself. With engineering helmed by [[Ashan Stonehand]] herself, an interconnected series of passageways funnels heated air and riverwater throughout to various rooms and fountains.

"You leave [[The Caverns]]' main hall through the door to the right of the stage, opposite where the stairway comes down. A few more rooms and offices line this passage as it winds deeper into the rock, but before long you exit through a pair of swinging wooden doors to a thoroughfare. Above you hangs a vibrantly painted red sign with "The Caverns" written on it in bold happy letters. To your left you see limestone formed into a large basin, full of water which seems to be streaming out of a channel in the tunnel wall to keep it continuously filled as a grandmother Aire and two young children fill water skeins and clay jugs. Rivka, you were here earlier today as you recall a small hostel with horrible coffee just two lefts and a right away.

The passage ahead has a slight rightward curve and stays about 10 Chazzes wide and tall as it and extends through a series of smooth round archways which line the streets like ribs. The rock itself has a ridged and wavy look to it as if this whole network was constructed with dripping wet sand, but at a touch it feels solid and porous, like any other limestone you know.

As you walk down the street, following the flow of people, you notice considerably more Aire than in the other districts you've toured--the guild quarter, old city, town center, and fish market. Whereas most of Harport has this sort of commercial thrum to it, these streets move at an almost lackadaisical, nonchalant pace as the residents of the Birdfoot move from one ineffectual appointment to another. A brush of moving air whisks across your skin carrying the scents of sweat, smoke, and shit, alongside undercurrents of fresh coffee, hearty food, and burning dry herbs. You turn and recognize another one of those leather flaps modulating the flow of air through a series of small ducts in the rock. This whole place seems engineered, haphazardly engineered but holding together.

Smaller passageways branch off every hundred feet or so. Robin Ave leads up a wide and shallow stairway towards the surface, then Finch Ave seems to descend to a lower level. You pass Owl Ave and Duck Ave, Loon Ave and Rook Ave, Gull Ave and Hawk Ave ... "
