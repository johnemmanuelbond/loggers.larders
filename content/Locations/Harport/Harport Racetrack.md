###### Parent: [[Harport]], [[Birdfoot]]
The largest building in Harport, the racetrack is one of the city's main draws. Owned and operated by [[Frank Horibert]], and his #2 [[Simon Redmont]], here the liquid flows, meaning money. Mostly civilians losing it.

## Locations

### Casino

Two large floors of numerous tables and bars for playing games of skill and chance. Every form of card and dice game is practiced here, as well as stations for bookies to collect bets on the weekly horse races. The interior of the casino is lit by numerous chandeliers and braziers, as well as sunlight from the many windows facing the track. While the front of the casino stays very bright, the back can get especially dim at night. The back side of the casino is reserved for the various kitchens and employee-only rooms but these are low-security as the vault is in the guardhouse and any large payouts are covered by courier.

On a DC 13 perception/investigation check, the players can notice that the interior walls are a bit thicker than they really need to be, and judging by various small doorways in low-traffic locations, they probably contain hallways.

#### Cashier Stations
Well-dressed waitstaff wearing expressionless porcelain masks with painted flowers exchange cash for chips. The chips are thin pieces of stained wood, stamped with various images of flowers. 3 cashiers on the first floor exchange for Green, Brown, and Red chips, whereas 1 cashier on the third floor exchange for Brown, Red, and Purple chips. Each cashier is also accompanied by a guard.

#### Games
###### Three-Dragon Ante
- randomly determine dealer
- players bet, then deal out 5 cards each.
- players raise, call, or fold, then reveal a card
- player with the highest total wins.
###### Life or Death (Rollies)
Player vs house: High roll winds
- Tie: Surrender and lose half the bet, or go to war and double the bet to roll again.
- Push your luck: After winning you can bet everything. If you win the house pays double.
###### Copper Slots
Clockwork devices which spin little copper cylinders on an axel (within a glass case). Player inserts 1-9 copper coins (not chips): Roll 5d6 and count matches:
- 3 of a kind: 2-1 payout
- 4 of a kind: 4-1 payout
- 5 of a kind: 10-1 payout

#### Performances

The house band, the blindnotes, usually occupies the main theatre with upbeat improvisational dance music. Though occasionally operas and plays come through. There are also three babboons (Mimi, MooMoo, and MaoMao) trained to juggle and otherwise be funny.

#### Third Floor
[[Frank Horibert]]'s personal chambers and dining room for feting guests. Includes a lookout over the entire campus and all the amenities an oligarch could ask for. Decadent red curtains with golden threading cover the numerous windows, a large bearskin rug is on the floor of the main parlor, leather stools sit in front of a private bar with numerous beautifully decorated bottles of wine and liquor on display.

Entering the third floor requires an escort by a staff member with a tattoo. [[Simon Redmont]] personally approves every solicitor and holds a magic mirror which can detect the magical aura on the tattoo for frauds, but he is the only one with such a mirror.

### Guardhouse
A long and low stone building on the far side of the racetrack grounds, the guardhouse houses not only the living quarters for the guards, but also a locked vault for managing casino payouts, and a floor of in the underground basement, only accessible via ladder through Moser's office.

#### Vault

The vault doot is locked by *arcane lock* and can only be opened by a key in the possession of [[Frank Horibert]], though he frequently hands it off to [[Simon Redmont]] or [[Moser Wormwool]] to handle large payouts. The Vault door is made of stone on a steel hinge. It can be forced open with a DC 30 athletics check, or by destroying the hinge: (AC 18, damage threshold 15, 50hp, immunity to poison/psychic damage)

## Wedding "Heist"

The whole racetrack is decked out in yellow and red, yellow ribbons and draping hang from every building and even the trees, groundskeepers are handing out a flower for everyone at the entrance. You hear excited chatter about the upcoming race and how the winner gets the honor of bearing the lady [[Ophelia Berendsen|Ophelia]] to the altar set. Glancing past the thrum you see the field in the middle of the track is covered in daffodils and pink tulips and red roses and in the center of that field there is a bare white dais circled by pristine whitewashed empty benches.

While most of the guards are busy patrolling the wedding, they may recognize the party.

The party makes a group check upon trying to gain entry (stealth / deception / persuasion). If more than half of them meet each threshold, certain effects happen:
8 - The party gains entry, but raise 1 suspicion level
12 - The party gains entry

Suspicion: Measured on a scale of 1-6. It starts at 1. The following things increase suspicion
- Caught by a patrol outside of wedding-related locations
- Casting a spell with verbal/somatic components in clear sight
- Being seen with a visible weapon
- Indiscretely asking around about esmellia
- Showing the trout pin
- Making a scene
- SPECIAL: Wildshaping or de-wildshaping in *anyones* view is +3 sus

Based on suspicion the players encounter a patrol of guards upon entering each of the following locations if they roll a 1 on the patrol die:
- Each casino level.
- The Thoroughfare
- The bandstands
- The flower garden

If they players encounter a patrol, they get one round of actions to do something (talk, hide, run, fight) with a DC based on the suspicion table. A failure ends with an escort back to the lcoation given on the suspicion table. For suspcicion > 3, they are escorted with enough guards to outnumber the party 2-1. IF suspicion > 5, Moser will try and confiscate their weapons and throw them in the cells.
