###### Parent: [[Harport]], [[Fish Market]]
The sound of shouts and chatter are the first things that enter your ears as you turn a corner and are greeted by one of Harport's most merry and most odd traditions. Smells of freshly fried dough, candied apples, souring milk, freshly scaled fish, and plenty of sweat assault each of your noses. In this quadrant of the neighborhood the lengths of fishing line and net that drape between homes are decorated with all manner of banners and lanterns decorated with silhouettes of fish, and bread, and bricks, and dramatic masks, and person's initials, and trees, and stars. A true cross-section of the city sits in front of you, a menagerie of various humanoid forms mingle in the central fare of this impromptu market. You see people in fine dress to disheveled rags, with jubilant to fearsome expressions, carrying obscured bundles, woven baskets, small purses, and open weapons. A few [[Aire Nomads|Aire]] children grin wide as they dart between some of the numerous and colorful stalls and carts that line the outside of this square, they're followed quickly by a similarly laughing human boy yelling "I'm it", who's trailed by an exasperated older man who's similar red hair and sharp nose eases your worry. If you listen past the shouts of barkers, and peek beyond the brightly advertised vendors down the many alleys that jut out from this square, you see business, in vitro. What is your business here, tonight?
