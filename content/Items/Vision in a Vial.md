### If he wants them back

1. Intersperse memory with horror
- "You remember this strange metallic taste in your mouth, it had been a long day and you're so thirsty"
- "Your son takes off running, is it towards you or away?"
- "This memory is hard, you see so much of your wife in him. Exhausted, with pitted eyes,  limply reaching up at you as you hold a bloody mass in your arms."
- Scenes of thrashing, tearing, and drinking; all in first-person

2. Reality fades away
"With lust sated the fuzz recedes, and the only sound left in your home is the the metronomic drips of blood falling from the corner of your mouth into the stinking and thickening pool at your feet. You're unsure how long you stand there before the rhythm is finally broken, by a single teardrop, mixing into the puddle like a silver swirl amongst the oxidation and coagulation. You look down at this spiraling cloud of red and black and white, feeling outside your own body. The room starts to spin, you can't tell if you're looking up or down, the pool below you is impossibly far off and yet right at your feet"

3. Push forward or pull back?
"The only thing left in your periphery is the last light of the setting sun as it finally drops through the doorframe. It seems to vanish, eclipsed by that great bloody disc at your feet. I'll ask you now, does John Moore stand there and look deeper into the dark, or would he pass out from the intensity?"

4. If pass out:
"you wake up with a dab of blood under your nose, and a conviction of unknown origin: __ will be back... after all, you are."

5. If push onwards:
"Like an eye opening through a crack in the heavens, that roiling pool at your feet pushes aside four pebbles, white, grey, blue, and orange, as easily as your son would have once kicked a rock down the road. It dominates your entire perception, chaotic and angry, focusing your attention towards its center, a single black spot amongst a writhing cacophony of red eddies and whipping white haze. The eye of an immense storm, or is the storm the eye itself? The eye... of the storm... is the eye... of the storm... is the eye... and it's looking right at you."

"Suddenly you're ripped up out of your skin, bones left behind, and hurtled up and up towards this great disc. You glimpse a throne before a gate. On the throne sits a figure. Massive, horned, facing in all directions, undulating and collapsing in on itself before permeating this entire space, like the roiling clouds all around you. You're forced prostrate before it's seat and the last thing you feel is it's unimaginable gaze, regarding you as a great leviathan would regard a krill, before you are drawn asunder and dispersed."

"Something heard your prayer for your son. You get the feeling he'll be back... after all, you are."

### If he wants them to rest

- You're reminded of an old parable about rivers
	- something like how individual droplets fall and life blooms as they move through the world
	- before long they're picked up by a creek, then a stream, then a river, forming connections with all the other water droplets
	- They might tarry in a lake, or in a tree, but eventually every droplet flows out into the open ocean.

Magic can perform all kinds of wonders, bloom beautiful flowers or grow tall trees, light a thousand candles or catch a raindrop before it hits the ground. It can even stop the flow of rivers. Likewise the [[Osisran Monks|Monks of your order]] can catch a spirit before it touches the world, or temporarily reverse its motion. But to take a spirit back from a long death would be like trying to collect a specific raindrop from the ocean. Impossible, and anathema. But that droplet made life, and the life left behind will persist and propagate throughout the entire world in ways too intricate to predict.

In this moment you know your family isn't really gone, no one can leave this place any easier than a raindrop can will itself out of the ocean. They are, and always will be, real.

### Production notes

cosmic horror techniques
- Admission: State plainly that the thing is ineffable
- Circumscription: describe the effects rather than the thing itself
- Magnification: Compare the thing to something already extreme
- Alienation: Describe how it violates principles of reality
- Combination: use a combination of analogies to create a liminal effect
- Obfuscation: be vague and ambiguous
- Abstraction: use nonsensical descriptions
- Disorientation: reference alternative mind states
- Fictionalization: lie and exaggerate
- Omission: give no description
