#### Background

**The Lemming Prince:** You were a well-known individual within a lemming colony located nearby to ancient [[Elga Elluin]]. Being cleverer than all the other lemmings you excel at playing any challengers against each other and remaining the most influential lemming. Despite your own talents you could never quite win the heart of the lemming princess, who had everyone in the colony fawning over her grace and poise and whiskers. In an attempt to impress this princess you steal baubles and trinkets from the tall folk, they have such pretty things and their guardians are so easily bamboozled. There's a supernatural flavor to your uncanny ability to escape a cat. During one of your escapades you glimpsed a figure of surpassing gaudiness, and on his finger was this indescribably shiny and succulent metal band, it would make the perfect tiara for your princess. For weeks you waited for your opportunity, you followed him around the stone warrens and arboreal nests of the tall folk, through a wall of light and into a dimly lit stone cavern.

**Cursed to a Human Form:** It was far too late when you finally accepted that you were trapped. The wall of light had vanished behind you, and as you wandered the halls of this odd structure you began to lose track of just how long you had spent there. Occasionally you'd catch a glimpse of that tall man who led you to this maze of lost identity. He'd acknowledge you, smile that evil, calm, insulting smile, and work his fingers into a sleeping spell. Slowly, unsettlingly, each encounter changed you a little. First, the smells dimmed as your nose shrank into your face. Then, the air chilled as your coat fell to the floor. Finally, the ceiling shrunk to meet you as as you found yourself wanting to stand on two legs.

**Frozen Underground (WIP)**: One day, the stone chamber you had been huddled in began to shake, and the the man--now frantically running from room to room--pointed at you with a look of horror and ... *flash!*  Your memory flashes white and you wake up here, in this dingy and moss-covered burrow, surrounded by people you've never seen before speaking in a strange dialect of a language you only barely know.
