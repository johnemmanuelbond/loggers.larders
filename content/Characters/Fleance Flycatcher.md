###### *Male, [[Andradorans]], 34*
###### Location(s): [[Harport]], [[Milton]]
A scholar on the run from [[Silverspire]], Fleance has uncovered ancient and dangerous knowledge. He tries to protect the innocents from the magical dangers that man has wrought.

Fleance is often cloaked with his face hidden by a double-brimmed brown hat. But underneath you'd see a gaunt man with pale skin, yellow eyes, and jet black hair. He is missing the ring finger on his right hand and walks with a limp. While his voice is occasionally raspy, his words are kind and his intonation is genuine.

#### Stolen Book
Once checked out a book from an [[Osisran Monks|Osisran]] monstary: The book was a theory about how some cults on [[Drudwythe]] seem to worship a second face of [[Pan|Morian]]. This, darker, god has similar aspects, like the role of escorting spirits and an association with sleep and peace. But this aspect may grant his grace to pull a spirit back with the promise of servitude thereafter. The book focuses on common symbols between the more common Morian, and this [[Pan|Charn]] from Drudwythe, a similar role in myth as a gnawing and smoothing force on the world, and the implication that it's watching over the world from a darkened moon.

#### Relationship with Esmellia
Fleance and [[Esmellia Leek]] bonded as they shared a ship to [[Harport]] out of Silverspire. While Fleance hasn't shared much of his research with Esmellia, she does know the broad strokes: cultures appear to be long-connected, maybe the [[Aire Nomads]] have similar connections.

In addition to just being friends with similar anti-imperialist politics, they have agreed to watch each others' backs in the event of [[Andradora|Andradoran]] bounty hunters.

##### Personal Research and History
Fleance is pulling at a thread he was never supposed to. As the son of an ambassador, Fleance has always been driven by a desire for knowledge. In his youth he spent significant time in [[Capital City 2]], and while his family brought an [[Andradora|Andradoran]] tutor with them, Fleance eagerly lapped up [[Drudwythe|Drudwythan]] knowledge.

In particular, Fleance studies the cross-connections of myth and history. His first foray into this field came when he first returned from Drudwythe to pursue university in Silverspire. His unusual knowledge of Drudwythan culture (including some stolen histories of shadebinding) reared its head when he started noticing odd parallels between Drudwythan myth and the codified [[Mardwythe|Mardwythan]] histories. In both cultures, old deities have common elements, similar heroes have similar nemeses, and a deep-rooted fear of the ocean prevented maritime development for many long centuries.

Pulling at these threads brought Fleance to the attention of the [[Knowing Shepherds]], whose influence subsequently Fleance away from the favor of his professors. Fleances ideas about common cultural origins did not fly in a culture of Andradoran primacy, and so Fleance had to pivot his thesis towards Drudwythan geopolitics in order to graduate. Fleance did enjoy a productive, though very brief, career at Silverspire's preeminent university before his more out-there ideas got him quietly removed.

Holding a grudge, Fleance has associated more and more with the Knowing Shepherds and had even started funneling Andradoran discoveries to Drudwythan scholars (for the benefit of worldwide academic unity, of course). Fleance was eventually found out, and luckily the Knowing Shepherds were able to tip him off before any formal charges could be brought, and so Fleance ended up on a boat to [[Nordwythe]] with nothing but ideas to guide his next steps, well ideas and a stolen book concerning the [[Osisran Monks]].
