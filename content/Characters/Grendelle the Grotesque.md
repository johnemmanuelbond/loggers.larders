###### *Female, [[Aire Nomads]]/[[Harport Colonials]], 199*
###### Location(s): [[Grendelle's Valley]], [[Milton]], [[Harken's Sound]]
The young "Daughter" of the [[Old Hag]]. The trueborn daughter of [[Shae]] and [[Harken Keelmender]] was in fact kidnapped from the first andradoran settlement after Harken's initial expedition. Since the Old Hag's slaying, her spirit has haunted/tutored the Grendelle into the malevolent force she is today. Obsessed with revenge, Grendelle lurks in the southern forest forging monsters and plotting to bring down everything Harken wrought.

#### Appearance
With a hunched back, swampy green nettled skin, and a hooked nose, Grendelle is the very image of disgust. Her black pupiless eyes look deep through many veils and many gnarled yellow teeth lie missing. White hair matted with mud and willow leaves sprouts out of a green shawl wrapped around her head and shoulders. Her spindly, triple jointed arms hang by her side, ending in sick and fungus-infested claws for fingernails? Her black cloak does nothing to hide simlarly fungal toes as she stomps through the swamp, scattering fauna before her.

Her dog Toto, however, is a delight

#### Early Years
Born in [[Weir's Point]] to Shae and Harken, Grendelle's true name is Hagatha Shaesdottir.

#### Malevolent Magics
Intensely curious about the ancient [[Ancient Merlings|Merling]] tongue, she is inspired by the beasts she sees emerge from the deep lake. Killing and studying them has informed her own use of spirit magic to make the abominations she plans to unleash. This fascination with the Merlings and the [[Shay-Luuq]] started when Grendelle robbed a bestiary off of an [[Alberta de Genne|explorer]] that she left for dead after a fateful encounter in the forest. Deciphering the ancient language based on conversations with the nearby spirit at the [[Grendelle's Valley|Sealed Tomb]], she has used this ancient knowledge to hatch a plot.

#### The Skinsplinter Plot
Grendelle's association with [[Harald Brightbrow]] began as most of her encounters do, with a murder in the woods. The victim, in this case, was a rather undistinguished Solton soldier, whose much luckier companion escaped Grendelle's claws. The 'lucky' soldier didn't last much longer since his tall tales of a horrifying woods woman sculpting the flesh of valiant men into a morbid abominations caught the attention of Gransmaster Harald, who, for the sake of more, cut this loose thread. By then Harald was already deeply ensnared by the spirits of the ruin under Solton, and looking for aid in comprehending the ancient Merling theological writings, and so he reached out to Grendelle.

Grendelle was as surprised as Harald to find a kindred spirit on the opposite side of the sound (and of the law). As a test of competence, Grendelle tasked Harald with obtaining a skinsplinter seed before she would divulge the ancient knowledge she had deciphered so far. Harald obliged.

Grendelle's cruelty and Harald's obsession have since fed off each other, what was initially a gauge of dedication has blossomed into a veritable scheme. Grendelle, using the knowledge gleaned from her Beastiary, will awaken the Skinsplinter vines in the middle of [[Milton]]'s [[Festival of Sole Moon|autumn festival]] and sow the ground with colonist blood. Blaming the southern [[Aire Nomads|Aire]] for the disaster, Harald will finally have enough support to ramp up military presence in [[Harken's Sound]]

Meanwhile, Harald's agent [[Tievel Set|Tievel]] will be gathering anti-Aire elements to further build out Harald's power base so he can eventually make a play for control of [[Harport]] itself. Beyond the destruction of [[Harken Keelmender|Harken's]] descendents and all they hold dear, Grendelle is excited about the prospect of an exiled and vulnerable population of feedstock for her dark magics. She is also hopeful that [[Knights of Termat|Termat]] rule will be a little more welcoming to her due to her having facilitated that rule.

#### Lair Encounter
Approaching the lair the party will see several mangled corpses and skeletons, as well as many crows perched... watching.
- A few of the crows will have one foot missing.
- most of the corpses are normal animals, though there is at least one Aire who will have fairly nice hide armor, a battleaxe, and a filcher's fork (a traditional Aire sling, 1d6 ranged 40\/100 ft). All have feet and hands missing.

Additionally several magic mouths will trigger, upon entry to the grove the party hears odd sounds, a deep grumble and the sound of a flock of birds taking off
- DC 13 perception or insight reveals the birds haven't gone anywhere, and in fact, they're intentionally staying out of sight

At the center of a grove lies a massive willow tree. Hanging from the bows of this trees are various creatures hooves claws and talons, and even a few Aire and what look like dwarven... hands.

Upon entering the grove a magic mouth bleats "welcome trespassers" in a scary hag voice.

Nearer to the tree there are two notable corpses in metal cages, a saber-deer and an alligator.
- Each of these purple and sloughing and bloated with the same odd fishlike features that the party recognizes.
- They appear dissected, as opposed to harvested, and their limbs are all intact

Also under the tree is a familiar altar and several cauldrons with brewing tools scattered about on wood and stone shelves, and a ratty old cot, no standing structures however.

Grendelle herself will be disguised (DC 20, though maybe discernable by touch) Aire corpse near the base of the tree. The tree of hands will attempt its grapple and disarm as long as the party is underneath it. Cutting down the hands will take several hours, and Grendelle will open with enemies abound should they try this.

The players may drink from the cauldron and roll a d6:
- on a 1-2 they are poisoned for a minute.
- on a 3-4 nothing happens
- on a 5-6 they turn invisible for a minute or until they attack or cast a spell. If another player rolls this for the second cauldron they'll get enlarge\/reduce (enlarge if &gt;10 on d20)
- Pouring the cauldron on a creature deals 2d6 fire damage, but none if they succeed a dex save.

The altar has familiar bloody runes, a comprehend languages can read out the phrases: "vines that thirst" "thorns that rake" "when gods align"

A DC 13 religion or arcana check reveals that \"when gods align\" refers to the [[Festival of Sole Moon]]

Grendelle is going to attempt to incapacitate the party with damage and *sleep*. Once disarmed and captured we'll probably end the session on a cliffhanger.

If there's a ton of time left then Grendelle will monologue about how she knows all about them and they can't stop her plans to poison the river, and no magic plant that Milton may seed will save it (this is a lie, of course, the plant is Grendelle's goal after all).

Captured = grappled by the tree of hands with the most problematic party member's dominant hand tied to a chopping block (Grendelle is walking around with a bloody axe). She may use suggestion to gather intelligence about party questgivers and might mutter something about killing [[Esmellia Leek]]. Alternatively, the party can try to drive her off with attacks, after which they'll have full run of the place.

#### Lair Investigation
If the party drives Grendelle off, start a 60 minute timer, (after which Grendelle returns to get her stuff).

Above ground, there is the Altar, the Cauldron's with brewer's tools, and the cot. Periodically, Grendelle's magic mouths yell out for help, a DC 10 perception check gives a vague direction as down, seemingly muffled by the earth. The party can find a tunnel downwards by looking under the cot, or after 2*(20-Investigation check) minutes.

The party can squeeze down the tunnel, which is lined with slippery willow roots, if they want. Failing a DC 8 athletics check causes a player to fall in, and take 1d6 damage.

Inside is a large cavern lined by more of those willow roots, some of which have sconces attached with tallow candles lit inside. The center of the room is dominated by a magical circle and a familiarly mutated corpse of a large wolf.

There is a desk with a few books against one wall. Various specimens sit in jars and cages pushed up against the wall. Hung on the far wall is an oil painting of a [[Shae|beautiful Aire woman]] with dark hair, striking brown eyes, and garbed in pristine leather armor. However, under the moonlight it turns into a painting of an [[Old Hag|old crone]] with a horribly bent nose, green skin, and piercing red eyes underneath sickly strands of white hair coming out of a black shawl.

Laying open on the desk is a magic tome, written in that language from the tomb, open to a page with a drawing of a red viney mass erupting from a pool of blood under one multicolored moon. Comprehend languages reveals that this book is a beastiary, and the open page is titled "Skinsplinter Vines" a monstrous plant creature created by the author's enemies, [[Northern Elves|the elves]], which can only awaken when the spheres' light reflects off a pool of blood sitting on top of where the seed was planted. When given more blood the plant grows and grows, until it destroys entire villages. The author notes native spiritualists have been able to curb and heal the land afflicted by this species of plant. Seeds of this plant are inert unless magically activated in a special ceremony with the blood of a "[[Shay-Luuq]]" (possessed animal)

Additionally the party can find other things on an investigation check:
- 10:
	- 2 vials of thick brown liquid (healing potions)
	- 1 cages of aire hands, 2 cage with saber deer hooves, 2 cages with lake newt feet.
	- A vial of eyes.
	- An inkwell that makes one a little nauseous if you stare at it. The ink vanishes when written onto parchment, and is only visible under moonlight.
	- A couple of empty pages. (letters she was writing to her patron about making an example of Milton and spreading fear).
	- A compass.
- 13:
	- 1 more roll on [table]("https://chartopia.d12dev.com/chart/410/)
	- The voices are coming from even further below.
	- A phial labeled "[[Mork]]"
	- A potion of fire breathing (orange smoking liquid)
	- A tunnel leading down into the newt burrows.
- 15:
	- remnants of burned parchment, and a discarded red seal (the same as they saw earlier) on the floor
	- A scentless vial of clear liquid. (but to [[Rivka]] it smells strongly of ginger)
	- A mummified raven's claw in the false bottom of a desk drawer
	- the currently inactive smoke trap:
	- a hidden enclave behind a willow root with a potion of mind reading (misty violet liquid)
	- a potion of invulnerability (unnaturally still, golden liquid).

If the party leaves before 60 min then Grendelle will recover anything they don't take. Otherwise she's going to invisibly sneak down (stealth check) trigger the smoke trap, taunt the party, and seal off the entrance by pouring one of the scalding cauldron's down into the hole (dealing 2d6 fire damage if someone is in the tunnel). And the willow roots on the tunnel start to come alive, increasing the difficulty to climb up the tunnel to DC 15 athletics

Smoke trap: A creature can hold its breath for 1+con minutes. If the creature speaks or breathes it takes 1d4 poison damage and must make a DC 11 con save. On a failure the creature's max HP is also reduced by that amount, and it spends a turn wretching.

The smoke has nowhere to disperse but settles after an hour. It will follow the party down into the newt tunnels, but the party will outrun it if they move quickly enough.

Upon descending into the newt tunnels, a DC 10 investigation check finds the magic mouths.
