
#### Visions of Madness
Upon drinking the [[Vision in a Vial]], John Moore is asked to recall the last time he truly felt like himself. He describes the feeling of being lost in the woods, seemingly without an identity. He comes upon a farmhouse and sees a father leading their son back home. Observing them for a while he identifies with the father as his face starts to meld into that very visage. Identity is meaningless and any face can be his. Tearing out of the woods he grabs the kid and runs off, that's his son, definitely.

Many weeks of near-starvation later the "father" and child come across a new farmhouse. Upon approaching the farmhouse, John is flooded with memories. Memories of coming home to a loving wife and a full pot, memories of a door creaking open in the dead of night. Memories of embracing family after a hard day's work, memories of wrenching faceless figures from peaceful sleep. Memories of a hearthlit meal interspersed with memories of tearing through flesh and sinew. John is left dissociative, somewhere between a memory and dream, as the drops of dripping blood mingle with the drips of dropping tears. He decides to sit and wallow in the darkness.

As he fades out of consciousness he watches the last light of the sun fall into the black cloud of blood at his feet. 4 pebbles, white, grey, blue, and orange, are swallowed by the pool as it swirls. Suddenly John is ripped and spun into a red and black storm. Wrenched through space and thought as he perceives an enormous gate, seated in front of which is a horned [[Pan|figure]] on a throne, peering in every direction and directly at him. He feels seen, and it does not feel good.
