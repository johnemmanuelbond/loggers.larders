###### *August 23, 0223*
Several Lumberjacks have gone missing from [[Milton]] over the past few weeks, most recently [[Frieder Harkensson]] carrying [[July's Locket]]. The party must venture through the logging trails to find the culprit.

### Instigation
[[Esmellia Leek]], do-gooder as she is, contracts a party of adventurers staying in the Startled Starling Inn in [[Milton]]. Offering to pay 32 gold to the four of them if they can find and retrieve [[Frieder Harkensson]] or his wife's [[July's Locket|Locket]].

### Exposition around town
Talking to [[June Sturgil]], or [[Moser Wilderly]] reveals that all the logger's found of [[Frieder Harkensson|Frieder]] was a left boot with a little slime on it.

[[July Harkensson]], [[Esmellia Leek]], and [[June Sturgil]] know about the cultural significance of [[July's Locket]] as a symbol of peace between the [[Aire Nomads|Aire]] and the [[Harport Colonials|colonials]].

[[July Harkensson]], [[Old Gran Mimsdottir]], and [[Moser Wilderly]] know about the legend of [[Harken Keelmender]] and the [[Old Hag]], and of the monstrosities let loose into the woods by her malevolent magics, little green men, wolves with fungal growths, headless human spirits who steal the face of their victim, etc.

[[Wilson Fit]] is simply concerned that he'll be forced to increase hours in order to compensate and doesn't want to seem the bad guy.

### The Boot Scene
The party has learned the boot was found on the western path near where the path leads away from Shae's stream.

Inv, Perc, Surv:
- DC 10: Only bootprints along the trail, the whole area (not just the path) seems to have intentionally been made up to look normal (all the bootprints are the same). Slime on a tree near the river.
- DC 14: The obfuscation was dragging marks towards the river. (debris was dragged from elsewhere to cover it up.
- DC 17: A body collapsed suddenly before it was dragged.
- Detect Magic: Slime has a very weak necromantic feel to it, and there seems to be some of it flowing from upstream back towards Milton.

### Dretch Fight
After following the river, the party will find the 6 pools.

Enter scene: Roll d6 and Stealth for the [[Dretch]], and for [[Frieder Harkensson]] (with adv for cover)

Perception:
- DC 10: See the altar and some bone piles, several of the pools are bubbling (narrow it down to three, one of which has the dretch). There's also a logger's axe stuck into a nearby tree.
- DC 15: Note the dagger in the ground.
Investigation: walk into the Dretch's trap: Once a PC walk's within 20ft of the Dretch, it uses Fetid Cloud on a surprise round, roll initiative: The Dretch tries to eat the tastiest person.
